Sources. History: DEMIG (2015) DEMIG VISA, version 1.4, Full Edition, Oxford: International Migration Institute, University of Oxford; bilateral visa requirements, annual January snapshots, 1973–2013. Later snapshots: Passport Index dataset, 2019–2025 (ilyankou, MIT). Two sources, two methods. The Passport Index source counts a broader set of arrivals as open, so its levels run higher; it is shown as a separate snapshot series, never spliced onto the older line, and the 2014–2018 gap between them is left empty. Map outlines: world-map-country-shapes (simplemaps, MIT).
How to read it. A country is coloured by its visa rules for incoming travellers, or by its passport's visa-free reach. Where the record has no entry, the country is hatched rather than guessed. Former states (the USSR, Yugoslavia, Czechoslovakia, the two Germanys, the two Yemens) are missing from the modern map but can be held as passports.
